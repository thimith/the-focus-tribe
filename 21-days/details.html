The 21-Day Focus Challenge: Full Details

You're here because you want the full picture before committing. Good. Read everything below. Then decide.

The Problem

Why You Keep Starting Over

You're not lazy. You're not undisciplined. You've proven you can start things. You've done it dozens of times. The problem is finishing.

Somewhere between day 3 and day 14, the novelty wears off. Life pushes back. The goal that felt urgent last week now feels optional. You tell yourself you'll restart Monday. You don't.

This isn't a willpower problem. It's a structure problem. When there are no stakes, no one watching, and no external pressure, quitting is costless. So you quit.

The most consistent people in the world don't have more willpower. They have better systems and people around them who expect results.

The Solution

A Small Group With Real Stakes

The 21-Day Focus Challenge is a structured accountability sprint. Not a course. Not a coaching program. Not a mastermind with dozens of strangers.

It's a tight cohort of 10 people max who all commit publicly, track visibly, and face real consequences if they don't follow through.

The timeline is fixed. The structure is simple. The expectations are clear from day one. Three weeks is long enough to build momentum and short enough to stay focused.

The Process

How It Works: In Detail

01

Onboarding Call

Before the challenge starts, you get a 1-on-1 call with Thierry. You'll clarify your goals, set your consequence (the thing you'll have to do if you don't complete the challenge), and make sure you're set up for success. This isn't optional. It's how you show up with intention instead of drifting in on day one.

02

Commit Publicly

On day one, you post your goals and your consequence to the group WhatsApp. No vague intentions. Specific, measurable commitments. The group sees everything. This is the moment it gets real. When others know what you're doing and what you're risking, the psychological stakes go up significantly.

03

Daily Tracking

Every day, you post your check-in to the group. Did you do it? Yes or no. 30 seconds. Everyone sees. There's no hiding a missed day. No "I'll catch up tomorrow." The daily visibility is what makes this work, light enough to not be a burden, present enough to matter.

04

Weekly Live Calls

Three calls over the 21 days: week 1, week 2, week 3. Video on. Everyone present. You share wins, obstacles, and what you're adjusting. Thierry facilitates and personally checks in on each person. These calls are where the community forms. Most cohort members stay in touch long after the 21 days end.

05

The Consequence System

Before day one, you choose your consequence. It needs to be something you genuinely don't want: a donation, a public admission, a payment. Something real.

We want you to succeed. If you slip, Redemption 1! Slip twice, Redemption 2!! Third time, you fail...

Deeper dives

Anything daily and specific: habits (exercise, meditation, reading), projects (write every day, make sales calls), creative work, health goals, business tasks. Up to 3 goals. We clarify these on your onboarding call to make sure they're achievable and measurable.

You choose before day one. It needs to be something you genuinely don't want to do. Past examples: donating to a cause you oppose, posting a public confession, cold showers for a week. The group holds you to it. This isn't about punishment, it's about making quitting as costly as continuing.

Each call runs 60-90 minutes. Video on. Each person shares their wins from the week, any obstacles they hit, and what they're adjusting. Thierry facilitates and checks in on each person. It's part accountability meeting, part community call. Most cohort members say these calls are their favourite part.
