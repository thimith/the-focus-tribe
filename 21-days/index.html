Common Questions

Q&A

A 21-day accountability sprint in a small group of 10–12 people. You define up to 3 goals, commit to them publicly with a real consequence, track daily, and join 3 weekly live calls with the full cohort. It's not a course. There are no modules to watch. It's a structure that forces you to show up.

Anything with a daily action. Fitness habits, business goals, creative projects, quitting something, building a routine — past cohorts have done all of it. The key is that your goal has a clear daily action you can track as done or not done.

You choose it. It has to be something real — money, a task you hate, something that genuinely stings. You state it publicly to the group on day one. If you miss a day three times, you pay it. No renegotiating. This is what makes it work.

30 seconds per day to log your check-in, plus 3 live calls of roughly 60 minutes over the 21 days. The time to actually do your goal is on you — the challenge just makes sure you show up for it.

You get two redemptions over the 21 days. Miss once — you earn a redemption. Miss twice — second redemption. Miss three times — you pay your consequence. It's structured to be firm but human. Life happens. Three misses means you didn't show up.

If you complete all 21 days, attend every call, and track every day — and honestly got zero value — you get a full refund. No hassle. That's the deal. If you quit halfway, there's no refund; the structure only works if you're in it.

Thierry. He's run multiple cohorts, personally tracks every member, leads all the live calls, and has been through the challenge himself. This isn't delegated to an assistant or automated — you'll actually have him watching and pushing you.
